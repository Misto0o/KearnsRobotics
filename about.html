FTC Robotics Overview

What is FTC?

FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) is a robotics competition for middle and high school students. Teams design, build, and program robots to compete in an alliance format against other teams. The goal is to inspire students to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

How FTC Robotics Works

FTC robots are built using a combination of commercial and custom components. Teams utilize a variety of materials, including plastic, metal, and electronics, to create their robots. The process involves:

  • Design: Teams sketch out their robot's design, considering functionality and strategy for the competition.
  • Build: Using metal parts, teams construct their robots, ensuring they meet size and weight constraints.
  • Programming: Teams program their robots using languages like Java, allowing them to autonomously perform tasks.
  • Testing: Continuous testing and iteration help improve performance and reliability before competitions.

Competition Format

In competitions, robots participate in a series of matches where they must complete various tasks. Points are earned based on performance and teamwork. Teams also prepare an engineering notebook documenting their design process and strategies, which is evaluated by judges.

Benefits of FTC Robotics

Participating in FTC helps students develop crucial skills such as teamwork, problem-solving, and technical proficiency. It fosters a passion for innovation and prepares them for future educational and career opportunities in STEM fields.
